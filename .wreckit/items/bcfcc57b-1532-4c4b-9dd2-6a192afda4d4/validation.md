## Executive Summary

**Recommendation:** 🔴 **Dismiss**

**Confidence Score:** 0.38 (Low)

**Verdict:** **Too narrow and undersized** market opportunity. VR gaming is a **small segment** ($15-25B) of the larger gaming market ($200B+), and the promotion/marketing sub-segment is **even smaller** ($200-500M). **Severe platform dependency** on Meta (Quest), Valve (Index), and Sony (PSVR2). **Content creators/influencers** already handle VR promotion organically. **No clear differentiation** from general game marketing platforms. **Dismiss** unless you have **insider VR industry connections** and are targeting **services business** rather than platform play.

---

## Market Analysis

### Market Size & Growth

**Total Addressable Market (TAM):**
- Global Gaming Market: $200-250B (2026)
- VR Gaming Market: $15-25B (6-10% of gaming)
- Game Marketing/Promotion: $10-20B
- VR Game Promotion: $200-500M (subset)
- **Conservative TAM estimate:** $200-500M

**Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):**
- VR game marketing services: $100-200M
- VR game discovery platforms: $50-100M
- VR influencer marketing: $30-50M

**Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM):**
- VR game promotion platform: $20-50M
- VR marketing services: $10-30M

**Growth Rate:**
- VR Gaming CAGR: 15-25% (solid growth from small base)
- Gaming Marketing CAGR: 10-15%
- **VR segment growing** but from very small base

**Market Reality Check:**
- **VR gaming is niche** - Only 6-10% of total gaming
- **Low VR headset penetration** - ~50-80M active headsets globally
- **Meta Quest dominates** - ~70% market share
- **Limited VR game releases** - ~500-1000 new VR games per year (vs 10,000+ flat games)
- **Low marketing budgets** - VR games have smaller budgets than flat games

**Conclusion:** **Very small market** with **limited number of potential customers** (VR game developers).

## Final Recommendation

### Confidence Score: 0.38 (Low)

**Recommendation:** 🔴 **Dismiss**

**Rationale:**

1. **Market Too Small** - $200-500M TAM for VR promotion is tiny. Even capturing 10% = $20-50M revenue max.

2. **Platform Dependency** - Meta and Steam control VR game distribution. They also control discovery. Building third-party promotion platform is fighting the platforms.

3. **Limited Customer Base** - Only 500-1000 VR games released per year. Most have tiny marketing budgets ($5K-50K).

4. **No Venture-Scale Returns** - Best case is small services business, not venture-backable opportunity.

5. **VR Gaming Still Niche** - Despite years of growth, VR remains small segment of gaming. May never become mainstream.

6. **General Platforms Can Add VR** - Steam, Meta, even Epic could easily add VR-focused promotion features.

**Only Viable Path:**
- **Services business** - VR marketing agency (not platform)
- Requires deep VR industry connections
- Not venture-scale

**Recommendation:** Dismiss. This is a lifestyle business opportunity at best.
